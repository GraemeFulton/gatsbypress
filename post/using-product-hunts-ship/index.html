Bootstrapping with Product Hunt Ship

Published: 2018-06-03

Here’s my quick review of using Product Hunt’s Ship feature as an indie maker.

My Email Otter product started off without a website or anything, just as some unstable code running on my Mac. Product Hunt’s free version of Ship saved me putting together a sign up form and define the product through their Upcoming Page:

Create a tagline

The tagline needs to sum up the product in a quick tagline, so helps you focus on the precise problem your solving.

Upcoming Page

The Upcoming Page helps you build a page to introduce your product, and offer people a way to sign up for updates:

  • Introduce yourself
  • Describe the product in few sentences
  • Explain why people should subscribe

Send Messages

The messages feature saves you signing up to an email service, and the free version shows some basic analytics, like open rates:

For free this is good! But to upgrade and get more features it costs around $50, which seems a bit too much for a bootstrapped product.

Also, I don’t think many people have actually discovered the product on the Product Hunt platform, but from me sharing it outside. So the main benefits so far have been the speed I could set up a coming soon page, and it’s got me 50 sign ups before this site even existed:

Overall, I think it’s great, and will continue using it alongside this blog. Their pricing makes sense if the product is already making money, but $50 a month before earning a penny myself makes no sense :/
